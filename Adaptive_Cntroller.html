Adaptive Augmentation Controller (ACC):

The Adaptive Augmentation Controller (AAC) is a feature-aware extension of Latent Generative Time-Series Augmentation (L-GTA) designed to improve forecasting performance on complex and heterogeneous time-series data, particularly in tourism demand forecasting. Unlike traditional augmentation methods that apply fixed transformations, AAC dynamically adjusts augmentation strength and type based on statistical characteristics of each time series, such as trend, variance, skewness, and seasonality. Built on a Conditional Variational Autoencoder (CVAE) backbone, the system generates high-quality synthetic sequences in latent space while an adaptive controller selects suitable transformations (scaling, shifting, noise, rotation) during training. Experimental results across tourism, retail (M5), and crime datasets show that AAC improves forecasting accuracy, robustness, and stability compared to static augmentation and standard generative models.
